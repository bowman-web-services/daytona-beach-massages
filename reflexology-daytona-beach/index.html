What is Reflexology?

Reflexology is an ancient healing practice based on the principle that reflex points in your feet correspond to organs, glands, and body systems throughout your entire body. By applying targeted pressure to these specific reflex points, a skilled reflexologist can stimulate healing and restore balance to your entire system.

This is not a regular foot massage. While a traditional foot massage focuses on relaxing the foot muscles themselves, reflexology works with a specific map of the foot that represents every part of your body. Our therapists are highly trained in reading the foot and applying deep, powerful pressure to the exact points that correspond to your specific health concerns.

The Therapeutic Principle: Zone Theory

Reflexology is based on zone theory—the idea that your body is divided into ten vertical zones, each represented on the feet, hands, and ears. When you stimulate reflex points through pressure, you activate natural healing responses in the corresponding organs and body systems. This helps restore energy flow, improve circulation, and support your body's natural ability to heal itself.

Our therapists understand the reflex map intimately and can target the exact points that align with your specific health goals, whether that's relieving digestive issues, reducing headaches, improving sleep, or balancing hormones.

How Reflexology Works

1. Reading the Reflex Map

Your therapist examines your feet to identify tender or sensitive reflex points. These points often indicate areas in your body that need healing or attention.

2. Applying Targeted Pressure

Using specialized thumb and finger techniques, your therapist applies firm, sustained pressure to the reflex points corresponding to your specific health concerns. This is deep, therapeutic work—not light tickling.

3. Stimulating the Healing Response

The pressure activates nerve endings and triggers the body's natural healing mechanisms. This increases circulation, reduces inflammation, and promotes balance in the corresponding body system.

4. Supporting Whole-Body Wellness

Unlike treatments that target one specific area, reflexology works on your entire body through the feet, creating comprehensive healing and balance.

What a Reflexology Session Looks Like

You'll be comfortably seated or reclined as your therapist examines your feet and begins applying targeted pressure to reflex points. The pressure is firm and therapeutic—this is deep, powerful work that goes beyond a typical foot massage.

Your therapist may ask questions about your health concerns, stress levels, and specific issues you're experiencing. They'll use this information to focus on the reflex points most beneficial to you.

You may feel sensations throughout your feet—sometimes tender, sometimes deeply relaxing. It's common to feel tingling or warmth radiating through your body as the reflexology activates healing responses. This is a sign the therapy is working.

Many clients report feeling deeply relaxed during the session, while others feel energized as their body's natural healing systems activate. After a session, you may experience improved sleep, enhanced digestion, reduced pain, or greater mental clarity within hours or days.
