What is Deep Tissue Massage?

Deep tissue massage is a therapeutic technique that uses slow, deliberate strokes and firm, sustained pressure to target the deep layers of muscle and connective tissue. Unlike lighter massage styles, deep tissue work reaches beyond the surface to address tension, knots, and restrictions in the muscle fibers themselves.

Our therapists use various techniques—including friction, compression, and trigger point work—to release chronic muscle tension and improve mobility. The pressure is intense but controlled, and communication with your therapist is key to ensuring the work matches your comfort level.

Deep Tissue vs. Swedish Massage

Swedish massage focuses on relaxation and general wellness, using lighter pressure and longer strokes to improve circulation and reduce stress.

Deep tissue massage targets specific problem areas with firm pressure and specialized techniques. It's therapeutic rather than purely relaxing—the goal is to release deep tension, break up muscle knots, and restore function.

Conditions That Benefit from Deep Tissue Massage

Chronic Back Pain

Deep tissue work addresses muscle tension and postural imbalances that contribute to lower and upper back pain.

Neck Stiffness & Headaches

Tight neck muscles often trigger tension headaches. Deep tissue therapy releases this tension at the source.

Sports Injuries

Accelerates recovery from muscle strains, sprains, and overuse injuries by improving circulation and reducing scar tissue.

Sciatica

Tight muscles in the glutes and hip region compress the sciatic nerve. Deep tissue release can reduce pain and numbness.

Fibromyalgia

Gentle but targeted deep tissue work helps reduce widespread muscle pain and improve sleep quality.

Muscle Knots & Trigger Points

Persistent tension spots respond well to focused pressure and specialized trigger point release techniques.

What to Expect During Your Session

Communication is Essential

Tell your therapist about pressure preferences, any injuries, and specific areas that need attention. During the massage, let them know if pressure is too light or too intense—they'll adjust accordingly.

Some Soreness is Normal

After deep tissue work, mild muscle soreness (similar to post-workout soreness) is completely normal and typically fades within 24-48 hours. This indicates the therapy is working at a deep level. Drink extra water to help flush out metabolic waste released during the session.

Cumulative Benefits

While you may feel relief after one session, chronic conditions often improve with regular therapy. Many clients benefit from weekly or bi-weekly sessions to build lasting relief.

Breathing & Relaxation

Focus on slow, deep breathing throughout your massage. This helps your body relax and allows the therapist to work more effectively into tight areas.
